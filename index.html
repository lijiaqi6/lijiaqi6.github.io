PhD Thesis Summary

My dissertation examines household time allocation across three chapters.

Chapter 1: Time allocation of mothers (Job Market Paper)
  • The literature puzzles over why Black women exhibit high labor-market attachment compared to white women with similar observable characteristics.
  • This paper shows that high-wage Black women return to work immediately after childbirth driven by expected risks in the labor and marriage markets.
Chapter 2: Time allocation of fathers
  • Research on child development has largely focused on maternal trade-offs between parenting and labor supply.
  • This paper documents that heterogeneity in fathers’ parenting time contributes substantially to inequalities in child health and cognition.
Chapter 3: Time allocation of children
  • Existing work documents that rainfall shocks often reduce schooling and cognition, emphasizing the role of opportunity costs of schooling as child labor.
  • This paper shows that road access and commuting is another important channel: droughts in Ethiopia reduce travel time, raise enrollment and time spent on after-school study, and lead to higher math scores.
